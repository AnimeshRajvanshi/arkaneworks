Heat Resilience Challenge

In the 2022 Heat Resilience Challenge at ASUâ€™s EPICS, I collaborated with a team to design heat mitigation solutions for Arizona mobile home residents, later independently developing a PC-inspired ventilation system.

The team analyzed portable ACs, misters, and solar panels using Analytic Hierarchy Process for cost-efficiency, while my independent design used SolidWorks to create a mobile home unit with PC fans for airflow.

Simulations in ANSYS, validated with colored smoke visualization, confirmed a continuous cooling loop, predicting temperature reductions in the prototype, designed with protective flaps for low-cost scalability.

This project reflects my ability to blend thermal engineering and sustainable design, addressing heat vulnerabilities in communities with practical, energy-efficient solutions, ready for future prototyping.
