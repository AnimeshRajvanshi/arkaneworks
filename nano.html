Nanoelectronics Ultrasonic NDT

The Nanoelectronics Ultrasonic Non-Destructive Testing (NDT) project develops a novel method for inspecting microscale components using high-frequency sound waves, aimed at enhancing quality control in semiconductor manufacturing.

My role involved designing the ultrasonic transducer system, leveraging nanotechnology to detect subsurface defects in chips without damaging them, a critical advancement for next-generation electronics.

This work combines my expertise in materials science and signal processing, delivering a prototype that improves defect detection sensitivity over traditional methods.
