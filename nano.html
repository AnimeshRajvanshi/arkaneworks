Nanoelectronics Metrology

I’ve been developing a workflow, since joining Arizona State University’s Celano Lab in May 2024, aimed at enhancing defect identification timelines in semiconductor chiplets through ultrasonic testing simulations. By proposing a scaled-up model with adjusted frequencies, I seek to simulate microscale features like 10-micron pitch hybrid bonds, potentially improving reliability analysis for advanced 3D packaging—though its effectiveness remains under investigation.

Problem Statement
Advanced semiconductor chiplets, such as those with 10-micron pitch Cu-Cu hybrid bonds, demand precise defect detection (e.g., voids, delaminations) for reliability. Ultrasonic testing at high frequencies (e.g., 1 GHz) offers fine resolution, but software like CIVA struggles with small scales (d = 10⁻⁵ m), where wavelength limits (λ = c/f) challenge viewport capabilities. This slows critical workflows, prompting innovative simulation strategies.

Approach
Building on scanning acoustic microscopy (SAM) experience, I identified CIVA’s resolution constraints while studying failure modes in chiplets. I propose scaling the model by a factor k, adjusting frequency to maintain wave physics, informed by elastic wave propagation principles. This ongoing work explores whether such scaling could overcome software limits, with theoretical validation still in progress alongside team literature reviews.

Design and Simulation
My proposed workflow scales the model by k (e.g., k = 1000, 10 μm to 10 mm), reducing frequency to
f_new = f_original / k
(e.g., 1 GHz to 1 MHz), preserving the ratio
d/λ
where
λ = c/f.
For 10 μm at 1 GHz (c ≈ 1400 m/s), λ ≈ 1.4 μm, so d/λ ≈ 7.1; scaled to 10 mm at 1 MHz, λ_new ≈ 1.4 mm, maintaining equivalence. Using CIVA NDT, I’m modeling linear elastic wave propagation via
∂²u/∂t² = c²∇²u
(u = displacement), with SolidWorks CAD for visualization, though simulations are still being refined.

Feasibility and Safety
The approach assumes linear elasticity,
σ = Eε
(E = Young’s modulus, ε = strain), typical in ultrasonic testing, but nonlinearities (e.g.,
σ = Eε + βε²,
β = nonlinear coefficient) may affect accuracy. Scaled frequencies align with CIVA’s range, potentially easing computation, yet transducer scaling and material behavior require further study. As a simulation-based proposal, it poses no physical risks, with feasibility hinging on ongoing validation.

Results and Impact
Preliminary simulations suggest potential for improved defect visibility, using reflection coefficients
R = (Z₂ - Z₁)/(Z₂ + Z₁)
(Z = ρc, ρ = density), but results are tentative. This work-in-progress aims to accelerate chiplet reliability analysis, with possible workflow enhancements still under evaluation. If successful, it could bridge software limitations, though its true impact awaits confirmation through continued research.
