Nanoelectronics Ultrasonic NDT

Since May 2024 at ASU’s Celano Lab, I’ve developed a novel scaling workflow for ultrasonic non-destructive testing (NDT) to enhance defect detection in 10-micron pitch Cu-Cu hybrid bonds for chiplet reliability.

I proposed scaling model dimensions by k=1000 (10 μm to 10 mm) and reducing frequency from 1 GHz to 1 MHz in CIVA NDT, maintaining d/λ ratio (~7.1) to simulate wave physics accurately.

Using SolidWorks for visualization and principles of elastic wave propagation (∂²u/∂t² = c²∇²u), I modeled defect detection, addressing software resolution limits for faster analysis timelines.

Ongoing research validates scalability against material nonlinearities (σ = Eε + βε²), aiming to bridge simulation gaps without hardware, enhancing semiconductor 3D packaging reliability.
