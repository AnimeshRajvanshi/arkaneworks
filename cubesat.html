Gorgon CubeSat

The Gorgon CubeSat, proposed at Arizona State University’s Sun Devil Satellite Laboratory (SDSL) in 2023, is a 12U satellite designed to combat ocean plastic pollution through hyperspectral imaging and innovative communication. As a mechanical engineering student, I played a pivotal role in subsystem integration and proposed a groundbreaking Starlink-based data relay to eliminate ground stations. This project, born from a nearly canceled initiative I revived, reflects my passion for aerospace innovation and environmental stewardship, tackling the global “Missing Plastic Problem” with space-based technology.

The CubeSat features a SWIR hyperspectral imager (1000-1700 nm, 50 m GSD, 50 km swath) for detecting macroplastics in a 500 km sun-synchronous orbit. I collaborated on specifying components like the Blue Canyon XACT-50 ADCS (<0.1° accuracy) and DHV Technology solar panels (25 W), using SolidWorks for conceptual design. My key innovation was integrating a modified Starlink terminal (Ku-band, 100 Mbps) for space-to-space data relay, reducing latency and infrastructure costs. This required extensive research into orbital dynamics and subsystem compatibility, ensuring a cohesive 12U platform.

Scaling from a 3U to a 12U design introduced power and thermal constraints. I addressed these by modeling thermal dissipation in SolidWorks, ensuring electronics stayed within -20°C to 60°C. The Starlink integration posed a compatibility challenge, as its Ku-band system was untested in space-to-space contexts. I used hand calculations and RF propagation models to validate a high-bandwidth link, aiming for reliable data transfer. Coordinating with SDSL teammates, I balanced payload mass (4 kg) and power (30 W), optimizing for a 1-year mission life in a debris-heavy orbit.

Though conceptual, the design underwent iterative refinement. I conducted trade studies to select the Unibap iX5-100 OBC for processing hyperspectral data, and GOMspace NanoPower BPX for battery reliability. My Starlink proposal required simulating orbital passes, confirming coverage with existing constellations. I also led subsystem integration, ensuring the imager, ADCS, and communication systems aligned via MIL-STD-1553 protocols. This process, driven by my leadership, extended the project’s life, allowing peers to gain hands-on experience and secure internships (e.g., Lockheed Martin).

The Gorgon CubeSat demonstrates my expertise in spacecraft design, systems engineering, and creative problem-solving. By targeting ocean plastics, it supports global environmental monitoring, with potential applications in climate research. My Starlink innovation could revolutionize CubeSat communications, reducing costs for future missions. Though not yet launched, the project positions me to contribute to aerospace advancements. Future steps include prototyping the communication system and securing a rideshare launch, potentially with NASA’s CubeSat Launch Initiative.
