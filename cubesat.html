Gorgon CubeSat

The Gorgon CubeSat, proposed at Arizona State University’s Sun Devil Satellite Laboratory (SDSL) in 2023, is a 12U satellite designed to combat ocean plastic pollution through hyperspectral imaging and innovative communication. As a mechanical engineering student, I played a pivotal role in subsystem integration and proposed a Starlink-based data relay to eliminate ground stations. This project, born from a nearly canceled initiative I revived, reflects my passion for aerospace innovation and environmental stewardship, tackling the global “Missing Plastic Problem” with space-based technology.

Satellite Showcase

The CubeSat features a SWIR hyperspectral imager (1000-1700 nm, 50 m GSD, 50 km swath) for detecting macroplastics in a 500 km sun-synchronous orbit. I collaborated on specifying components like the Blue Canyon XACT-50 ADCS and DHV Technology solar panels (25 W), using SolidWorks for conceptual design. My key innovation was integrating a modified Starlink terminal (Ku-band) for space-to-space data relay, aiming to reduce latency and infrastructure costs. This required extensive research into orbital dynamics and subsystem compatibility, ensuring a cohesive 12U platform.

Scaling from a 3U to a 12U design introduced power and thermal constraints. I addressed these by modeling thermal dissipation in SolidWorks, ensuring electronics stayed within operational temperatures. The Starlink integration posed a compatibility challenge, as its Ku-band system was untested in space-to-space contexts. I used hand calculations and RF propagation models to validate a high-bandwidth link, aiming for reliable data transfer. Coordinating with SDSL teammates, I balanced payload mass and power, optimizing for a 1-year mission life in a debris-heavy orbit.

Though conceptual, the design underwent iterative refinement. I conducted trade studies to select the Unibap iX5-100 OBC for processing hyperspectral data and GOMspace NanoPower BPX for battery reliability. My Starlink proposal required simulating orbital passes to confirm coverage with existing constellations. I also led subsystem integration, ensuring the imager, ADCS, and communication systems aligned via MIL-STD-1553 protocols. This process extended the project’s life, enabling peers to gain hands-on experience and secure opportunities.

The Gorgon CubeSat demonstrates my expertise in spacecraft design, systems engineering, and creative problem-solving. By targeting ocean plastics, it supports global environmental monitoring, with potential applications in climate research. My Starlink innovation could reduce costs for future missions, a tentative goal for CubeSat communications. Though not yet launched, the project positions me to contribute to aerospace advancements. Future steps include prototyping the communication system and securing a rideshare launch, potentially with NASA’s CubeSat Launch Initiative.
