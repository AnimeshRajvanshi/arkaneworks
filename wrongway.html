Mechanical Wrong-Way Driving Prevention System

The Mechanical Wrong-Way Driving Prevention System is a robust solution designed to mitigate the lethal risks of wrong-way driving on highways, developed during my Mechanical Engineering capstone at Arizona State University in 2024.

As part of a seven-member team, I collaborated on the design and prototyping of a speed hump system, taking sole responsibility for its electrical components to alert impaired drivers through tactile and electronic feedback, enhancing road safety without disrupting normal traffic.
