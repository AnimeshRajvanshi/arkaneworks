Wrong Way Project

The Mechanical Wrong-Way Driving Prevention System is a capstone project aimed at enhancing road safety by preventing wrong-way driving incidents. This system integrates a novel mechanical design with embedded electronics to detect and deter incorrect driving directions, validated through prototyping and testing.

Causes of Wrong-Way Driving

Wrong-way driving often stems from driver impairment (e.g., alcohol, fatigue), poor signage, or confusing road layouts. Our system addresses these by physically guiding drivers and alerting authorities, reducing collision risks. Data from the National Highway Traffic Safety Administration shows over 500 fatal wrong-way crashes annually in the U.S., underscoring the need for proactive solutions.

Mechanical Systems

The system features an active speed hump with depressible stainless steel ramps, designed using SolidWorks and validated with ANSYS FEA (safety factors 2.5-3.3 under 1000 lbf). An Arduino Uno R3 subsystem detects ramp depression order to determine driving direction, triggering alerts via LED signals and wireless communication to nearby authorities.
