Wrong-Way Interceptor

As part of my MEE capstone at Arizona State University (Januaryâ€“December 2024), I contributed to designing, machining, and assembling a mechanical system to prevent wrong-way driving. Using SolidWorks, I modeled an active speed hump made of rows of depressible stainless steel ramps, then ran ANSYS simulations to validate its durability and functionality. I built an Arduino Uno R3 circuit to detect driving direction by analyzing the order of ramp depressions, ensuring the system adapts dynamically to traffic. This project showcases my skills in CAD, simulation, and prototyping for a real-world safety solution.
