Lunar Power Grid

The Lunar Power Grid is an innovative energy delivery system designed for NASA’s Watts on the Moon Challenge, enabling sustained lunar operations under the harsh conditions of the lunar surface. By adapting a JAXA-Toyota Lunar Cruiser rover, my team engineered a robust solution to distribute power from solar plants to remote lander-based battery hubs, supporting NASA’s Artemis missions with reliable, scalable infrastructure.

Core Components
At the heart of the system is a modified Lunar Cruiser rover, retrofitted to deploy power cables across vast lunar distances. We integrated a custom cable-laying mechanism inspired by terrestrial trenchers, paired with modified lunar landers repurposed as high-capacity battery packs. Space-grade docking connectors link the network, ensuring seamless energy transfer between power generation and storage units.

Specialized Features
The cable-laying system, drawing from electric dog collar trenching tech, buries lightweight, durable cables beneath the lunar regolith to shield them from micrometeorite impacts and thermal extremes. Corrosion-resistant coatings protect both the rover and cables from abrasive lunar sand, while precision docking ports prevent contamination during connection cycles. These features combine to deliver a resilient grid capable of operating in the Moon’s vacuum and temperature swings.

Technical Highlights
The rover’s trenching arm operates with a deployment rate optimized for the Moon’s low gravity, laying up to 500 meters of cable per sortie. Each lander battery hub stores 100 kWh, sufficient to power Artemis habitats and rovers during lunar nights. Our design underwent rigorous simulations to validate performance under -173°C cold and abrasive dust conditions, ensuring uninterrupted power for future lunar bases.
