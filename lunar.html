Lunar Power Grid

Developed for NASA’s Watts on the Moon Challenge (2023), the Lunar Power Grid is a cable-laying rover system designed to deliver 100 kWh of power to Artemis mission sites, addressing lunar environmental challenges.

I collaborated with a team to design a trenching mechanism and solar-powered rover using SolidWorks, simulating performance in ANSYS under lunar conditions (-173°C, abrasive regolith) to ensure reliability.

The system tackles the Moon’s 14-day light-dark cycles and extreme temperatures, integrating heat pipes and insulation to protect cables, with a focus on cost-effective, scalable power delivery for habitats.

Though conceptual, the project demonstrates my ability to apply systems engineering and thermal analysis to space applications, aligning with NASA’s Artemis goals for sustainable lunar exploration.
